Crypto Jargon



What is FIAT currency?

FIAT is a government-issued currency that is not backed by a commodity (such as gold). FIAT currency gives central banks greater control over the economy because they can control how much money is printed.

So in the crypto space, FIAT currency is classed as any government-issued currency (USD, EUR, GBP) that is not a cryptocurrency.

What is USD Tether (USDT) and why is it so useful?

Tether (USDT) is a stablecoin cryptocurrency designed for each token to be represented by an underlying US dollar. Each USDT token is pegged to a US Dollar that is held in Tether Limited’s reserve balance and can be obtained through the Tether platform.

What is a Stablecoin?

A stablecoin is a new class of cryptocurrencies that attempts to offer price stability and are backed by a reserve asset. Stablecoins have gained traction as they attempt to offer the best of both worlds - The instant processing and security or privacy of payments of cryptocurrencies, and the volatility-free stable valuations of FIAT currencies. The leading and most widely used stablecoin is USD Tether (USDT.)

What is an Altcoin?

An Altcoin is any other digital currency other than Bitcoin or Ethereum. There are more than 3,800 altcoins listed on CoinMarketCap at the time of writing. Most Altcoin’s start out as the equivalent of penny-stocks, with a promising use case & great marketing. Yet, in most cases, these coins fail to prosper.

What is a Private Key?

A private key or passphrase is the base entry to access your entire wallet. It’s used as a last resort if other passwords are lost or forgotten, or if you have lost your cold wallet device. Anyone that has your private key or passphrase has complete and entire access to your funds, and it can be used on any computer or device, anywhere in the world. This means you should never keep your private key or passphrase on your computer, or any device connected to the internet. The best way to keep your private key and/or passphrase safe, is to write it down on paper, and store it in the safest place possible.

What is the difference between a hot wallet & cold wallet?

The difference between a hot wallet and a cold wallet is that hot wallets are connected to the internet, while cold wallets are not. Which means, if a hacker has the capacity to hack into your computer, they can potentially take send your cryptocurrency to themselves. With a cold wallet, there is no internet connected to your wallet, so it is impossible to hack you. A cold wallet is the safest way to keep your cryptocurrency secure.

Is it safe to keep my cryptocurrency on an exchange? (Kraken, Binance, Coinbase)?

To maximize your security, the short answer is no.

The biggest exchanges can be trusted, and they are very secure. They also hold insurances and backup funds in case a hack occurs. Yet, they do occasionally get hacked for millions worth.

So, when you hold cryptocurrency on an exchange, you do not hold the private keys to that wallet, which means you don’t “really” own it, they do. It’s similar to a bank holding your currency, yet exchanges are not backed by governments and if an exchange is hacked & liquidated, there will be no possible way to recover your funds.
